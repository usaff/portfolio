ER Wait Times Analysis

Project Date: December 2024 - January 2025
Author: Muhammad Yousaf

Project Overview

The project aims to optimize patient flow and reduce wait times in the hospital emergency room by analyzing key performance indicators.

The Dataset

The dataset includes:

  • Patient wait times before receiving medical attention
  • Hospital admission rates following ER visits
  • Demographic characteristics of patients
  • Hospital capacity and staffing levels

Data collected from 250 hospitals across 15 states, covering over 1.5 million ER visits from January 2023 to December 2024.

Key Findings

  1. Longer Wait Times Correlate with Lower Admission Rates
  2. Hospital Size & Staffing Levels Impact Wait Times
  3. Peak Hours Cause Severe Delays

Recommendations & Potential Solutions

  • Increase ER staffing during peak hours (6–8 PM)
  • Implement advanced triage systems
  • Set a 90-minute maximum wait time for non-critical cases
  • Use machine learning to predict daily patient volumes

Conclusion & Call to Action

Optimizing ER operations can significantly reduce wait times and improve patient outcomes. Hospitals should leverage data-driven strategies, adjust staffing based on demand, and invest in predictive analytics to improve efficiency and patient satisfaction.
